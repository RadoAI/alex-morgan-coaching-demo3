Dubai's business landscape is shifting. Rising costs, increased competition, and a flood of new market entrants are making it harder to stand out. But here's what most people miss: every market shift creates a gap — and gaps are where opportunities live.

The entrepreneurs I work with who are thriving right now share one common trait. They stopped reacting to the market and started making deliberate decisions about where to position themselves within it.

Three things I'm seeing work in Dubai right now:

The first is hyper-specialisation. Generalists are struggling. Specialists with a clear, narrow focus are winning clients faster and charging more. The market is big enough — you don't need everyone, you need the right people.

The second is relationship-first selling. Cold outreach is dying. Warm introductions, consistent visibility in the right rooms, and genuine relationship-building is what's converting in this market. Dubai is a city built on trust.

The third is pricing with confidence. The entrepreneurs raising their prices right now are finding better clients, not fewer. The Dubai market rewards positioning. If you're discounting to compete, you're playing the wrong game.

The current environment isn't a problem to survive. It's a filter. The businesses that come out ahead in the next 12 months will be the ones that used this moment to get sharper, not smaller.
